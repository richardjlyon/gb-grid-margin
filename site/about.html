About

Grid Margin is a sceptic's gauge of how reliable Britain's electricity grid really is — the live companion to The Energy Trap, and sibling to the Subsidy Clock. The Clock measures the cost; Grid Margin measures the risk: how little wind and solar deliver, how much the system leans on gas and imports, and how close it runs to its limits.

What this is

The wind rarely blows; when it doesn't, gas and imports carry Britain. This site reads that off the grid's own settled records, half-hour by half-hour, and live in your browser. It is written in the sceptic's voice — but it counts and contextualises, it does not catastrophise.

The bargain

The data and the methodology are open on purpose: open figures are hard to dismiss. The cost of that openness is discipline — every number here is a settled or live Elexon/NESO/DUKES figure, with no modelled values in this first version. Where a figure has a known bias, we disclose it on the page, not in a footnote: the wind capacity factor is a deliberate conservative lower bound, and its cross-year level is a denominator-mix artifact. Both are stated where the figure is shown.
